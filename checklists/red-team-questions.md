# Red-team your analysis - 4 questions

Before accepting an AI-assisted result, argue against it. Four questions in particular - the questions a hostile reviewer asks first:

## 1. What estimand did this actually answer?

AI gives you what you literally asked for. The estimand question makes sure that matches what your protocol or your science actually needs.

## 2. What diagnostic would make me distrust this?

A concrete falsification criterion. If you can't name one, you don't have a model - you have a fitting procedure.

## 3. What did the AI not suggest?

The AI is generative within its training distribution; alternatives outside that distribution are the human's responsibility.

## 4. What would I do if the result were reversed?

A pre-mortem on the conclusion. If you'd report the work the same way regardless of which direction the finding went, you don't have a directional finding - you have a fitting procedure dressed as a finding.

---

## For regulated work

These become a sponsor-reviewable checklist. Many SAPs already include something like this; making it AI-aware just means asking the questions in light of how AI helped produce the analysis.
