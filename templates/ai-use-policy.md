# AI Use Policy

A green / yellow / red policy template for AI assistance on this project (or course, or team).

Customize the categories below to fit your work. The point is that everyone — students, collaborators, reviewers — knows what's expected.

## 🟢 Green — Encouraged

Use AI freely. No disclosure required.

- Boilerplate and scaffolding
- Syntax and idioms in unfamiliar languages
- Format conversions (CSV → JSON, R → Python)
- Search-query brainstorming (not citation lookup)
- Prose smoothing on already-written text

## 🟡 Yellow — Allowed with review

Use AI, but verify the output and disclose if it materially shaped the work.

- Analysis code (verify against expected outputs)
- Database queries (test before running on production)
- Methods-section drafts (rewrite in your own voice; check accuracy)
- Literature summaries (**always verify citations** against sources)
- Worked examples and rubrics (review for pedagogical fit)

## 🔴 Red — Stay hands-on

Do not delegate. Your own judgment is the load-bearing decision.

- Architecture and system design
- Methodological choices (what method, what estimand)
- Core algorithms
- Interpretation and defense
- Curriculum judgment / pedagogical sequencing
- Diagnosing what just happened in the room

## Disclosure default

Disclose AI assistance when it materially shaped analysis, code, interpretation, literature synthesis, or substantive writing — not when it merely helped with formatting or syntax.

Whatever your SOPs, sponsor protocols, or journal policies require is the floor, not your judgment. When in doubt, **disclose the role, not the tool.**
